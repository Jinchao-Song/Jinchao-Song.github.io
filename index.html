Bridging remote sensing, urban science and data-driven methods

I am a Postdoctoral Researcher at the University of Michigan, working at the intersection of urban computing, remote sensing, and spatial data science. My research focuses on understanding urban systems — their structure, function, and dynamics — through the integration of heterogeneous geospatial data sources.

My work has been published in leading journals including Landscape and Urban Planning, Remote Sensing, Computers Environment and Urban Systems, and Environmental Health, accumulating over 800 citations with an h-index of 9.
