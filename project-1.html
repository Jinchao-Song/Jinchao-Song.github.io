Background

Urban functional zones (UFZs) are fundamental units that reflect the socio-economic activities and physical structures of cities. Accurate mapping of UFZs is critical for sustainable urban planning, transportation modeling, and environmental management.

However, traditional remote sensing approaches struggle to capture the complex social functions of urban land. This project introduces a novel framework that fuses physical attributes from very-high-resolution (VHR) satellite imagery with social attributes from points of interest (POI) data.

Animated visualization of urban zone classification

Methodology

The framework consists of three steps: (1) image segmentation using object-based methods, (2) POI density and type aggregation within each segment, and (3) random forest classification to assign functional zone labels.

We applied this method to Xiamen, China, classifying urban land into seven functional categories: residential, commercial, industrial, public service, transportation, green space, and mixed use.

Key Results

87% Overall Accuracy
7 Functional Categories
10m Spatial Resolution

Our approach achieved an overall classification accuracy of 87%, significantly outperforming methods that use VHR imagery or POI data alone. The integrated framework better captures the heterogeneity of urban functions.
